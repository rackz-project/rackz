# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in Rackz and its community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and technical approaches
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users
- Showing empathy towards other community members

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments — public or private
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Sexualised language, imagery, or unwanted attention
- Personal or political attacks unrelated to the technical work
- Any conduct reasonably considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behaviour and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any unacceptable behaviour.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, or to temporarily or permanently ban any contributor whose behaviour is inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any official communication channel — and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported by contacting the project maintainers. All reports will be reviewed and investigated confidentially. Maintainers who do not enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face repercussions as determined by other members of the project's leadership.
